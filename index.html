Our History

The Stark's of Winterfell can trace their lineage back to the first men. Our proud house has lived in this icy climate for hundreds of years. It is more than our furs and hot springs that keep us warm during the long winters, it is the need of our southern customers for our prized ice that keeps our northern hearts ablaze!

How our Ice is Made

For the commoners out there: ice is made from frozen water. Therefore in order to make the best ice we must start with the best water.

It may be known to some of you that parts of the Castle Winterfell are heated by water from a pristine hot spring. When this water has cooled down during its journey through the castle walls it is filtered through screens of fine linen and then stored inside insulated barrels.

Next the water is stored outside to freeze. The icy winds of the north see to this in a hurry. Within a day's time the water is frozen solid. Within three days the temperature is so cold that it will take a month sitting in the blinding sun for it to warm up enough to begin to melt!

After the three day freezing period the insulated barrels are loaded into an insulated carriage where they will begin their journey southward. We focus on fulfilling orders but always have extra barrels in case we come upon a Southern Inn or Castle with a need for Stark's Ice!
